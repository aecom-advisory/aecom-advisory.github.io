Overview

Demographic information, such as population, educational attainment, employment, and income set the foundation for understanding existing conditions in an area of interest. The primary data source of demographic information is the U.S. Census Bureau. The data is usually collected through the American Community Survey (ACS), which is conducted every year, (most recently available from 2024) or the Decennial Census (conducted every 10 years, most recently in 2020). Given the recency of data available, ACS is used in most demographic data collection processes. Longitudinal comparison for tracking changes over a time frame (usually 10 years or more) is also common.

Pulling Census data manually

  • Visit data.census.gov.
  • On the home page, click "Explore Filters" to open the advanced search menu.
  • In this example, assume we are looking for the total number of housing units in the state of California. Use the advanced filters in the left sidebar to select California (under Geography > State), and use the Topic filters to select Housing Units (under Topics > Housing > Housing Units). Click "Search".
  • Select the B25002 table, with the heading "Occupancy Status".
  • The table will open up, showing the total, occupied, and vacant housing units in California for the most recent year of data. The dropdown menu next to the table name allows for the selection of a different year of data. Once you have selected the required year, click the Download button (to the right of the dropdown) to download your data as a .xlsx or .csv file.
