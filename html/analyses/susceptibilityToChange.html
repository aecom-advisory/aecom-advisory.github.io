Susceptibility to Change Analysis

Calculate the redevelopment suitability of parcels based on assessed value, lot coverage, and age of structure.

Overview

A susceptibility to change analysis is done at the parcel-level and identifies parcels that are more likely to be redeveloped in the future (relative to other parcels in the area). Parcel data for this analysis will usually be obtained via the client's GIS portal or through a data request.

Methodology

This analysis typically operates on three variables: improvement value ratio, lot coverage ratio, and year of construction.

  • Improvement Value Ratio: The ratio of a parcel's improvement value to its total assessed value. This variable measures the percent of the total assessed value of the parcel that is attributable to the structure on the parcel. The higher the improvement value ratio, the less suitable a parcel is for redevelopment.
  • Lot Coverage Ratio: The percent of a parcel's total area that has structures. This is a ratio of the parcel's building footprint to the parcel's total area. The higher the lot coverage ratio, the less suitable a parcel is for redevelopment.
  • Year of Construction: The year of construction for the structure on the parcel. The newer the structure, the less suitable a parcel is for redevelopment.

The variables above are measured against predefined thresholds to calculate a parcel's susceptibility score. Below are the thresholds typically used for this analysis. Note that these thresholds may need to be adjusted to better suit certain study areas.

  • Improvement Value less than or equal to 20% of Total Assessed Value: Parcels with an improvement value ratio equal to or less than 20% receive one (1) point.
  • Lot coverage less than or equal to 20%: Parcels with a building footprint that is equal to or less than 20% of the parcel's total area receive one (1) point.
  • Structures older than 1980: Parcels with structures built before 1980 receive one (1) point. Vacant parcels (parcels with no structures) also receive one (1) point.

In total, a parcel can receive up to a maximum of three (3) points for a susceptibility score. Parcel scores are typically labeled as follows:

  • 0 - 1 pt: Low Susceptibility
  • 2 pts: Medium Susceptibility
  • 3 pts: High Susceptibility
