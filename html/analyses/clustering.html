Overview

Industry clusters are groups of 6-digit NAICS codes that come together to define a broad industry. Summarizing jobs, GRP, and other metrics by these clusters, across multiple years, helps understand employment trends and local specializations.

The primary data source for industry data is Lightcast.io. Lightcast is a proprietary data source that offers detailed labor market information at several geographic levels (Nation, State, Metro Area, County, ZIP Code).

Terminology

Term Description
NAICS Codes 2-through-6-digit codes that uniquely identify industries that operate in the U.S. For example, NAICS '11' is "Agriculture". Each additional digit adds specificity. NAICS '1111' is "Oilseed and Grain Farming". NAICS '111110' is "Soybean Farming". Overall, there are 20 broad 2-digit industries that capture over 900 more specific industries that are identified by a 6-digit code.
SOC Codes 2-through-5-digit codes that uniquely identify occupations in the U.S. For example, SOC "15-000" refers to "Computer and Math Occupations". SOC "15-1200" refers to "Computer Occupations". SOC "15-1210" refers to "Computer and Information Analysts". SOC "15-1212" refers to "Information Security Analysts". There are 23 broad groups identified by 2-digit SOC codes, with several hundred more specific occupations identified by 5-digit SOC codes.
Jobs Total number of people employed by an industry in a given calendar year.
GRP Gross Regional Product (GRP) refers to the total value of goods and services produced by an industry in a given calendar year. The term GRP is often synonymous with 'output', and is a reliable measure for tracking the productivity of industries, especially when combined with a jobs metric to produce an estimate of total output per job in an industry.
Location Quotient (LQ) Location quotients are measures of local industry specialization. Taking the example of the industry identified by NAICS code '11' (Agriculture) in Cook County, IL, the LQ of the industry for 2025 can be calculated as the ratio of local Agriculture employment to total local employment divided by the ratio of national Agriculture employment to total national employment. An LQ below 1 indicates less local specialization than the national average, while an LQ above 1 indicates greater local specialization. Note that the "reference" geography (the denominator) does not need to be the nation in all cases - LQs can be computed relative to any other geography, though using the parent State or the nation is typical. Read more about LQs here.

Methodology - Industry Data

Logging into Lightcast and pulling data

  • Before logging into Lightcast.io, visit the license checkout to check out a license with your AECOM email id. Credentials for accessing Lightcast will be sent over to the AECOM email id, and can then be used to log in.
  • Navigate to the "Data Download" tab. Fill the required fields to specify the geographies to analyze. For the NAICS codes, it is typical to use the group of all 6-digit NAICS codes.
  • Use the "Select columns" feature to select the specific columns needed. Please note that GRP is available only through the "Industry Table" report within the "Industry" tab.
  • Once the required geographies and industries are selected, run the report and download the data as a .csv file.

Lightcast data download interface

Analyzing the data

  • The downloaded data can be analyzed in one of three ways - Excel, in R/Python, or via the Advisory NAICS Cluster tool.
  • If analyzing data in Excel or R/Python, refer to the materials in the package available for download above.
  • If analyzing the data through the Advisory NAICS Cluster tool, refer to the instructions provided with the tool.
